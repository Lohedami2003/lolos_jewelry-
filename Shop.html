La Rose

1781 Ruby Studded Necklace

Originally commissioned in 1781, this ruby-studded necklace was crafted by Duke Albert Christopher in honor of his late wife, Deaconess Rosetta Christopher. His search for its precious materials began more than a decade earlier, in 1770, during a diplomatic visit to the estate of the Count of Kambaska. It was there, amid the Count’s gardens, that the Duke first encountered Aurora’s Kiss—a deep, velvety red dahlia whose richness and strength reminded him unmistakably of his beloved Rosetta.

Read More

Yet such brilliance was rare. The ruby—the only gemstone capable of matching the flower’s vivid hue— could be found neither in Kambaska nor in the Duke’s homeland. His pursuit carried him across distant lands and spanned many arduous years. When Duke Christopher finally returned in 1781, bearing the completed necklace, he was met with devastating news: Deaconess Rosetta had passed away in his absence. According to her headmaid, she had resisted death for as long as she could, sustained by the hope of seeing her husband one final time.

As the Duke gently placed the necklace into her cold hands, he whispered only two words:

"There Rose".

These words would later be translated into French as La Rose —a name that endures, entwined forever with love, loss, and devotion.

Lady Oluchi

2000 Freshwater-Glass-Waistbead

Crafted in 2000, Lady Oluchi was carefully created by 13-year-old Emma as a cherished keepsake for her older sister Oluchi, who was departing for the University.

In her tender innocence of childhood, Emma hoped that even amid new friendships and experiences, Oluchi might ocassionally glance at the delicate white glass pearls and fondly remember her little sister.

By 2010, Lady Oluchi had come to be regarded as an enduring symbol of a younger sister's unwavering love.

Natures Dance

1990 Gold-Plated Emerald Studded Bracelet.

Inspired by vibrant splendor ofthe Great Barrier Reef, Benin artisan Mr. Favour Osakpolor, crafted this exquisite piece following his first journey to Australia in 1989.

Titlted Natures Dance, it was presented to his beloved mother as a heartfelt gift on her 60th birthday. Through this creation, he expressed his deepest wish: that her remaining years on earth be as lively, colorful and breathtakingly beautiful as the reef that had so captivated him.
